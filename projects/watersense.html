waterSense

Description

The goal of this project was to create a device that will help consumers test public bodies of water for pollutants. This way the consumers can safely swim in public water without the risk of exposing themselves to harmful contaminants.

The first idea was to use a combination of sensors to test the water and compare it to a “normal” value that has been decided earlier. The sensors that should be included in the design are a pH sensor, a TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) sensor, a conductivity sensor, and a temperature sensor.

Reflection

During the four weeks devoted to this project, I can safely say I feel like a lot has been accomplished. I am proud of the final design and its functionality. Although some further improvements could be made, I realize that this lies beyond our time and budget. That is why I feel so satisfied with the final product.

One of the things I am proud of most is the calibration of the pH sensor, after testing it under numerous circumstances, I was able to extract a formula that converted the ADC (Analogue to Digital Converter) signal into a pH value. This formula has an R2 of 1, that means that the readings from the sensor have a 100% accuracy (disregarding the standard-deviation of the sensors).

I am also very satisfied with the way our cable management turned out. In the beginning it looked like a big mess, but after Tibi (one of my group members) took it home and worked on it, it turns out there is a lot of space left. Because of the clear acrylic shell, it was easy for other people to look at our product and see exactly how we achieved our functionality.
