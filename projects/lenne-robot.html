LENN·E Robot

Description

LENN·E Robot is a school project, it is based of the RobotAtFactory Lite challenge. The goal of the project is to build a robot which is capable of navigating a pre-defined course. The course is charted by black lines on a white canvas. There are colored boxes on one side of the course, these boxes are placed in a random order, they must be identified by the robot based on their color, and relocated to the other side of the course and their associated position.

The robot is equipped with a plethera of sensors, e.g. ultrasonic sensor, color sensor, line tracking sensor. By applying some clever programming, the robot is able to navigate the course and identify the colored boxes.

Reflection

I was very excited to start this project; unfortunately not everybody appeared as excited as I was, which quickly slowed our progress. During the project we had numerous setbacks and annoyances. Our robot was accidentally dropped by one of the teammates, destroying most of the 3D printed parts and the line tracking sensor. The 3D printed parts were easy to replace, but the line tracking sensor could not be delivered in time before the final test. This resulted in us failing at the first attempt, luckily for us the line sensor got delivered soon after allowing us to continue testing and optimizing the robot.

In the end we managed to make the robot work mostly as intended, only it had some issues recognizing the junctions at which it needed to turn. Although I am not enterily satisfied with the end result, I do think I learned a lot from this project.
